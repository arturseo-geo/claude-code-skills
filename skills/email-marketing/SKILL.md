## Email Sequence Types

### Welcome Sequence (3-5 emails, 7-14 days)

**Goal:** Introduce brand, set expectations, drive first action

**Template:**
1. Email 1 (immediate): Welcome + chief benefit + CTA
2. Email 2 (day 3): Social proof + objection handling
3. Email 3 (day 5): Free resource/lead magnet
4. Email 4 (day 7): Social proof + urgency (optional)
5. Email 5 (day 14): One-on-one offer (optional)

### Nurture Sequence (ongoing, 2-4 emails/month)

**Goal:** Build trust, educate, prepare for sales

**Content mix:** 70% value, 20% education, 10% promotion

### Product Launch Sequence (5-7 emails, 14-30 days)

**Goal:** Build anticipation, handle objections, drive conversions

**Timing:**
- Day 0: Teaser (problem-agitate)
- Day 3: Video/social proof
- Day 5: FAQ/objection handling
- Day 7: Launch day email
- Day 8: ASAP bonus
- Day 14: Last chance
- Day 21: Post-launch content

### Re-engagement Sequence (2-3 emails)

**Goal:** Win back inactive subscribers (30-90 days no open/click)

**Approach:**
1. Email 1: "We miss you" + incentive
2. Email 2: "Last chance" + value
3. Final: Remove if still no engagement (ISP warmlist protection)

## Copywriting Formulas

### Subject Line Formulas

- **Open loop:** "You won't believe what..." (curiosity)
- **Question:** "Are you making this mistake?" (engagement)
- **Number:** "5 tactics for X" (scannability)
- **Social proof:** "27,000 creators tried this..." (credibility)
- **Urgency:** "Last day: [benefit]" (time scarcity)
- **Personalization:** "[First name], here's your shortcut..." (relevance)

### Email Body Formulas

**AIDA:**
1. Attention — Hook (problem recognition)
2. Interest — Benefit statement (why care)
3. Desire — Proof (testimonial, data, example)
4. Action — CTA (one clear next step)

**StoryBrand:**
1. Character — Reader (not you)
2. Problem — Specific, relatable
3. Guide — You as expert
4. Plan — Clear steps
5. Call to action — Specific CTA
6. Success — Outcome visualization

## Deliverability Best Practices

### Authentication (required)

**SPF (Sender Policy Framework):**
```
v=spf1 include:sendgrid.net ~all
```

**DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail):**
- Generated by email platform
- Add to DNS as CNAME record
- Enables 250+ reputation

**DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication):**
```
v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:admin@domain.com
```

### List Hygiene

1. **Remove bounces** — Hard bounces (invalid) immediately, soft bounces after 3 attempts
2. **Sunset policy** — Remove unopened/unclicked for 180+ days
3. **Re-engagement first** — Attempt 2-3 emails before removal
4. **Monitor unsubscribes** — Unsubscribe rate >0.5% signals issue

### Warm-up Protocol (new domain)

**Week 1:** Send to 50 engaged subscribers, monitor opens/clicks
**Week 2-3:** Increase to 500, watch for spam complaints
**Week 4:** Increase to 5,000, verify 0 complaints
**Week 5+:** Full list

**Kill signals:** >5% bounce, >0.1% complaint, replies to "unsubscribe"

## Analytics & Optimization

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| Open Rate | 20-40% | 15-20% | <15% |
| Click Rate | 2-5% | 1-2% | <1% |
| Bounce Rate | <2% | 2-5% | >5% |
| Unsubscribe | <0.5% | 0.5-1% | >1% |
| Complaint Rate | <0.1% | 0.1-0.3% | >0.3% |

### Optimization Priorities

1. Fix high bounce rate (list quality issue)
2. Improve subject lines (test 3 variations)
3. Optimize send time (test per segment)
4. Simplify CTAs (one clear action)
5. A/B test content (body, images, length)
