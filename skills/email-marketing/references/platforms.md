# Email Marketing Platforms

## ConvertKit / Kit

**Best for:** Creators, solopreneurs, course creators

**Pricing:** $29-$1,299/month (based on subscriber count)

**Key features:**
- Automation sequences (visual builder)
- Segment by content + engagement
- A/B testing (subject, send time, content)
- Tags for detailed segmentation
- Native landing pages
- Integrations: Zapier, WordPress, Gumroad

**Strengths:**
- Creator-focused (not B2B)
- Excellent email design builder
- Tag-based segmentation is powerful
- Good deliverability (dedicated IP available)

**Weaknesses:**
- No SMS included
- Limited dynamic content
- Expensive for large lists (>50k)

**Setup:** OAuth on thegeolab.net WordPress

## Mailchimp

**Best for:** SMBs, simple campaigns, budget-conscious

**Pricing:** Free (0-500 contacts) → $500/month (250k contacts)

**Key features:**
- Campaign builder (drag-drop)
- Basic automation
- A/B testing
- Segmentation (basic)
- SMS (paid add-on)
- eCommerce integration

**Strengths:**
- Free tier generous
- Simple, intuitive
- Good for SMBs
- Decent deliverability

**Weaknesses:**
- Automation limited (no conditional logic)
- Segmentation not sophisticated
- Open rate tracking less reliable
- Reputation issues historically

**Setup:** API key in dashboard

## ActiveCampaign

**Best for:** B2B, complex automation, enterprises

**Pricing:** $19-$229/month (contacts-based)

**Key features:**
- Workflow automation (visual builder)
- Advanced segmentation (custom fields)
- Dynamic content personalization
- Conditional email sending
- SMS + multichannel
- Lead scoring
- Sales CRM integration

**Strengths:**
- Most powerful automation
- Excellent segmentation
- Best-in-class personalization
- Strong deliverability
- Native CRM

**Weaknesses:**
- Steeper learning curve
- More expensive for small lists
- Setup more complex

**Setup:** API key, webhook configuration

## Beehiiv

**Best for:** Newsletter writers, Substack alternative

**Pricing:** Free → $600+/month (based on revenue share or flat rate)

**Key features:**
- Newsletter-first design
- Referral program (built-in)
- Boosts (paid promotion network)
- A/B testing
- Segmentation (audience + engagement)
- Analytics (better than Substack)

**Strengths:**
- Beautiful newsletter templates
- Built-in referral/growth features
- Writer-friendly interface
- Better analytics than Substack

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited automation
- Segmentation not as sophisticated
- Not ideal for B2B

**Setup:** OAuth with Gmail/Outlook

## Substack

**Best for:** Simple newsletters, micropayments, writers

**Pricing:** Free + revenue share (10% on paid subscriptions)

**Key features:**
- Simplicity (minimal features)
- Paid subscriptions (micropayments)
- Comments/engagement
- Mobile-first design

**Strengths:**
- Dead simple
- Built-in monetization
- Good writer community
- Strong deliverability (Google/Yahoo partnership)

**Weaknesses:**
- No segmentation
- No A/B testing
- No automation sequences
- Limited integrations
- Revenue share takes 10%

**Setup:** Sign up, connect social
