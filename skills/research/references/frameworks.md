# Strategic Research Frameworks

## SWOT Analysis (Strategic Planning)

**Purpose:** Assess internal capabilities and external market conditions

**Template:**

```
STRENGTHS (Internal, positive)
✓ What we do better than competitors
✓ Unique assets, team expertise, brand recognition
✓ Cost advantages, technology, network effects

WEAKNESSES (Internal, negative)
✗ Where we lag competitors
✗ Resource constraints, technical debt, brand perception
✗ Geographic limitations, team gaps

OPPORTUNITIES (External, positive)
→ Market gaps, emerging demand, underserved segments
→ Regulatory tailwinds, technology shifts, partnerships
→ M&A targets, geographic expansion, new use cases

THREATS (External, negative)
⚠ Competitor moves, market consolidation
⚠ Regulatory headwinds, technology disruption
⚠ Price wars, channel shifts, economic downturn
```

**How to use:**
- Strengths + Opportunities = Growth strategy
- Weaknesses + Opportunities = Investment areas
- Strengths + Threats = Defensive strategy
- Weaknesses + Threats = Risk mitigation

## Porter's Five Forces (Competitive Analysis)

### 1. Threat of New Entrants

**High barrier (good for incumbents):**
- High capital requirements
- Brand loyalty
- Switching costs for customers
- Proprietary technology/data
- Regulatory licensing
- Network effects

**Low barrier (threat to incumbents):**
- Low startup costs
- Easy customer switching
- Commoditized inputs
- No patents/IP moats

**Question:** How easily could a startup disrupt this market?

### 2. Bargaining Power of Suppliers

**High power (good for suppliers, bad for margins):**
- Few suppliers
- Switching costs high
- Supplier can integrate forward
- Input critical to business
- Supplier can differentiate

**Low power (good for customers):**
- Many suppliers
- Commoditized inputs
- Easy switching
- You can integrate backward

**Question:** How much pricing/quality power do input suppliers have?

### 3. Bargaining Power of Buyers

**High power (good for buyers, bad for margins):**
- Few buyers (concentrated)
- Buyer can integrate backward
- Product commoditized
- Switching costs low
- Buyer size large relative to seller

**Low power (good for sellers):**
- Many buyers (fragmented)
- Product differentiated
- Switching costs high
- Buyer size small

**Question:** How much pricing/quality power do customers have?

### 4. Threat of Substitutes

**High threat (bad for incumbents):**
- Alternative solutions exist
- Customer switching costs low
- Substitute price-competitive
- Substitute performance improving
- Substitute brand recognition high

**Low threat (good for incumbents):**
- No realistic alternatives
- Switching costs high
- Substitute more expensive
- Substitute performance inferior

**Question:** What alternative solutions exist to solve customer's problem?

**Example:** Video calls substitute for business travel (high threat to airlines)

### 5. Competitive Intensity

**High intensity (bad for profit margins):**
- Many competitors
- Competitors of similar size
- Slow industry growth
- High fixed costs
- Commoditized products
- Exit barriers high

**Low intensity (good for profit margins):**
- Few competitors
- Clear market leader
- Fast industry growth
- Low fixed costs
- Differentiated products
- Exit barriers low

**Question:** How much price competition and rivalry exists?

## Jobs to Be Done (Customer-Centric)

**Core concept:** Customers don't want products; they want to get a job done.

**Example:**
- Job: "Get milk home from store"
- Solutions: Jug, carton, delivery service, subscription
- Context matters: Traveling? (carton). Family of 5? (jug). Busy professional? (delivery)

**Research methodology:**
1. **Identify the job** — What outcome does customer want?
2. **Map the journey** — What's the process (before, during, after)?
3. **Find pain points** — Where does current solution fail?
4. **Discover opportunities** — How could you solve the job better?

**Output:** Job-outcome matrix, customer journey map, opportunity gaps

## PESTLE Analysis (Macro Environment)

**Purpose:** Assess external factors affecting the market

**Categories:**
- **Political** — Regulations, trade policies, government spending
- **Economic** — Interest rates, inflation, currency, employment
- **Social** — Demographics, cultural trends, consumer behavior
- **Technological** — Innovation, automation, digital disruption
- **Legal** — Compliance, litigation, licensing
- **Environmental** — Climate, sustainability, resource scarcity

**Template:**
```
POLITICAL: [Risk: tariffs, Brexit, tax changes]
ECONOMIC: [Risk: recession, inflation, cost of capital]
SOCIAL: [Trend: aging population, remote work]
TECHNOLOGICAL: [Opportunity: AI, automation, blockchain]
LEGAL: [Risk: GDPR, data privacy, labor laws]
ENVIRONMENTAL: [Pressure: ESG, carbon tax, water scarcity]
```

## Market Segmentation (Divide & Conquer)

**Purpose:** Identify distinct customer groups with different needs

**Common dimensions:**
- **Geography** — Country, region, urban vs rural
- **Demographics** — Age, gender, income, education
- **Firmographics** — Company size, industry, revenue
- **Psychographics** — Values, lifestyle, risk appetite
- **Behavioral** — Usage rate, purchase frequency, loyalty
- **Use case** — Problem being solved, context

**Analysis:**
1. **Size each segment** — Addressable market $
2. **Growth rate** — Fastest/slowest growing
3. **Profitability** — Margin potential
4. **Accessibility** — How easy to reach/serve
5. **Defensibility** — Moat strength

**Output:** Segment ranking by attractiveness, go-to-market per segment

## Value Chain Analysis (Where's the Profit?)

**Purpose:** Understand where value is created and captured in industry

**Steps:**
1. Map all value-adding activities (upstream to customer)
2. Estimate margins at each step
3. Identify bottlenecks/power points
4. Find margin expansion opportunities

**Example: SaaS**
```
Marketing → Sales → Onboarding → Support → Expansion
  (CAC)     (S&M)   (PSA)       (CS cost) (LTV increase)
  40%       25%      15%          20%         50% expansion revenue
```

**Insight:** If CAC high, focus on reducing Sales % or increasing LTV
